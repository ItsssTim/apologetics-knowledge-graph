The question

Does the changed life — forgiveness, peace, the moral rebuild of the convert, the reality of love itself — count as evidence that God exists?

Where Kalam and fine-tuning argue from cosmos to cause, Q-1.5.5 argues from the inside out: from the observed and lived transformation of human beings to the God claimed to cause it. It is the argument most experts say actually converts people — Lennox states flatly that “most people don’t believe in God because of an argument” — even as it is the one most exposed to the parity objection, since every religion and many secular programmes also change lives.

The corpus treats it not as a standalone proof but as a cumulative, falsifiable, publicly checkable claim: Christ makes a testable promise (forgiveness, peace, new power), the promise is observed to come true across decades and thousands of cases, and the best explanation of that data is the God who made it.

The cumulative case — evidence streams, not a syllogism

Unlike the Kalam (a deductive syllogism), this is an abductive cumulative case assembled from converging streams of human experience. No single stream is decisive; the cumulative weight is the argument — and several streams share the same parity vulnerability.

Stream A · Love beyond matter

  • Love is a decision of the will, not biochemistry
  • Enemy-love breaks the feelings-only model
  • “More than matter and energy” → a Creator
  • Longing for life after death (Dostoevsky, Woody Allen)
  • Carried by Cliffe Knechtle

Stream B · Transformation at scale

  • 60–70 years of pastoral observation
  • Narcotics, despair, broken marriages reversed
  • “It is supernatural when you see that happen”
  • The 500-lives falsifiability wager
  • Carried by John Lennox

Stream C · Autobiography

  • The apologist’s own changed life as data
  • Strobel: drunk in the alley → remade
  • Lennox: the Nobel-laureate career threat
  • Perry: Saul-to-Paul, told unvarnished
  • Strobel, Lennox, Perry

Stream D · Grace-radicality

  • The least-likely convert is more evidential
  • Evel Knievel, Khmer Rouge torturer, Saul
  • Merit-logic predicts no rescue
  • Believe + receive = become (John 1:12)
  • Strobel, Perry

Stream E · Miracle as datum

  • Paraplegia reversed in a moment
  • “No medical explanation” (doctors)
  • Fits the definition of a miracle
  • Yet alone did not settle faith
  • Carried by Wes Huff

Stream F · Encounter over argument

  • Pure evidentialism is “insane” (Kierkegaard)
  • God seeks us; we respond
  • Pascal’s Memorial — encounter, not proof
  • “Heart change only God can effect”
  • Craig, Perry

How each expert deploys it

John Lennox8 transcripts · dominant
Testability + the 500-lives wager
Signature architecture
  • Christ’s claim is a falsifiable hypothesis: “Jesus makes a claim you can test — trust him and he’ll give peace with God, new life and new power.” Answers the “Christianity isn’t testable” taunt head-on.
  • The data at scale: across 60–70 years, people from “narcotic dependence, broken relationships, feeling suicidal” remade. The “look at me” Chinese student on the balcony is his signature university closer.
  • The 500-lives wager: “I’ll bring 500 transformed by Christianity, you bring 500 transformed for atheism, and then we’ll talk.”
  • The energy analogy: “We scientists cannot even tell you what energy is” — yet we observe it. Observation without full explanation is still evidence.
  • Experience precedes argument: “Most people don’t believe in God because of an argument.” The illiterate boxer Stan Ford finishing the parable of the sheep and goats proves God “meets people where they are at.”
  • The Nobel-laureate dinner: ordered “in front of witnesses” to give up his faith or lose his science career — “What have you got to offer me that’s better than what I’ve already got?” It “put steel into me.”
Cliffe Knechtle4 transcripts
Love as more than matter and energy
Signature moves
  • Love is a decision of the will, not a feeling — “not because your sex drive propels you, not because you have a drive to the genetic pool.” Enemy-love is the proof a feelings-only model fails.
  • Love’s reality points past matter: “the only way there can be more to reality than matter and energy is if there is some type of God.”
  • Personal datum: the basketball coach he could not stand — “I experienced a power outside of myself to love” him.
  • Longing for the afterlife (Dostoevsky; Woody Allen) corroborates the resurrection promise — “death is not a friend, death is an enemy.”
  • Hostile-witness move (vs Aron Ra): “The morality you just espoused is exactly what Jesus taught” — turning the atheist’s altruism into Christian inheritance.
Stuart Knechtle variant

The Shakespeare–Hamlet analogy — “God relates to us like Shakespeare relates to Hamlet, the author who came down into the story” — plus the two-definitions-of-faith move (blind belief vs “counting up the evidence and putting your trust in a person”).

Lee Strobel2 transcripts
The changed life as eyewitness data
Distinctive deployment
  • The drunk-in-the-alley before-picture — self-incriminating credibility. The Alison-shutting-the-door vignette: a toddler hiding because “Daddy’s going to be drunk again.”
  • The five-year-old as second-order witness: “never studied a scholar… but she could watch” — then “I want God to do for me what he’s done for Daddy.” Inverts the “you didn’t know me before” problem.
  • Grace-radicality: Evel Knievel the narcissist; the Khmer Rouge torture-centre director now “telling everybody about Jesus.”
  • The scandalon embraced: pressed on Hitler / Sammy the Bull — “Yes. That’s exactly right… none of us is good enough.” Radical grace is the demonstration-feature, not the embarrassment.
  • Believe + receive = become (John 1:12) — his memorable formula.
Preston Perry3 transcripts
Transformation as testimony & encounter
Distinctive register
  • Spoken-word testimony: the Saul-to-Paul arc — “like Saul I had a brick for a heart that I often stoned Christians with… but like Paul I met Jesus.” The unvarnished before/after is the evidence.
  • Refuses the sanitised version: “Don’t speak of me perfect — tell them I wasn’t always a saint.”
  • Providence as evidence: surviving the Chicago projects, read against Nathanael’s “can anything good come from Nazareth?”
  • Encounter over argument: “Christianity is an experience, an encounter… not behaviour moderation but a heart change” — “no man can see God unless he unveils their eyes.”
William Lane Craig1 transcript
The limit of evidentialism
Distinctive move
  • Kierkegaard’s 70-year-old student — who masters philosophy, comparative religion, theology, Greek, Hebrew and Aramaic before converting at 70 — is “insane.” “Faith cannot hold its breath while reason sifts and resifts the evidence.”
  • God is the seeker: “A loving God would not abandon us to our own devices — he would himself pursue us.”
  • Pascal’s Memorial — “fire… certitude, certitude, feeling, joy, peace” — the decisive moment is encounter, not argument.
System frame

This is the frame that keeps the node from collapsing into rationalism: evidence is ministerial, the Spirit’s drawing and personal encounter are decisive. “Arguments can help, but ultimately we come to grips not with arguments but with the living Lord himself.”

Wes Huff1 transcript
The healing as honest datum
Distinctive move
  • Instantaneous healing from paraplegia: “I woke up, got up, walked over to my wheelchair and sat down — it was the doctors who said we have no medical explanation.”
  • It “qualifies” as a miracle by definition — supernatural, naturally inexplicable.
  • The honest concession: even that experience did not make him “rock-solid.” He still “had intellectual issues” and had to “square up the dots” — experience and argument are complementary, neither sufficient alone.

Objections handled — cross-expert master list

Objection 1

Love is just brain activity

Love is electrical and chemical signals; knowing the mechanism doesn’t diminish the experience, and appealing to God as an external source adds nothing.

Matt Dillahunty

Strongest response

Love-as-will is not reducible

Knechtle: choosing to love an enemy, against all feeling, cannot be generated by a sex-drive or gene-preservation account. Love is a decision of the will, not a biochemical urge.

Knechtle enemy-love argument partial — asserts, does not refute the reductionist account

Objection 2

That’s evidence of desire, not an afterlife

Dostoevsky wanting to rejoin his dead wife shows longing, not that an afterlife exists. Transformation testimony confuses wish with proof.

Matt Dillahunty

Strongest response

The resurrection, not the desire, is the evidence

Knechtle reframes longing as data corroborating a promise independently attested by the historical resurrection. Lennox stages transformation as a falsifiable claim plus observed data — not a wish.

Knechtle resurrection anchor Lennox falsifiability framing

Objection 3

The exemplar is a fraud

Mother Teresa “was in love with suffering,” not an agent of love — she withheld medical care because suffering brought people closer to God (Hitchens, The Missionary Position).

Matt Dillahunty / Christopher Hitchens

Gap — not engaged

Left standing

No expert in the corpus defends Mother Teresa or rebuts the charge. The counter-exemplar is simply not answered.

Aggregation gap

Objection 4

Cross-tradition parity

Buddhist, Muslim, Mormon, AA and secular-therapy conversions transform lives just as dramatically. So transformation cannot uniquely evidence the Christian God.

Standard sceptic

Gap — the central unmet challenge

Asymmetry implied, not established

Lennox’s 500-lives wager implies a difference of scale, and he distinguishes Christianity elsewhere by its falsifiable historical claims plus present forgiveness — but the like-for-like transformation parity is never directly defeated.

Lennox scale + historical distinctives parity not defeated

Objection 5

Selection / survivorship bias

The failures and the dead don’t testify, so any survivor will “feel” providentially preserved. The anecdotes are filtered by who is left to tell them.

Standard sceptic

Gap — unaddressed

Named in-corpus, answered nowhere

Both Perry’s providence testimony and Lennox’s anecdotal data are vulnerable to this; no expert rebuts it.

Aggregation gap

Objection 6

Message-without-metaphysics

Maybe belief in Christ’s teaching helps the marriage; he needn’t actually exist or have risen for the message to do its work.

Tse (Dartmouth Veritas Forum)

Gap at point of contact

Not answered in-transcript

Lennox does not engage the rebuttal directly. The implicit corpus answer — the resurrection decides whether the message is binding revelation or human wisdom — is not stated where the objection lands.

Aggregation gap

Objection 7

Why still doubt after a miracle?

A profound supernatural experience — doctors saying “we don’t know what happened” — should make faith rock-solid.

University questioner (to Huff)

Strongest response

Honest concession

Huff agrees the expectation is reasonable, then reports the lived reality: experience did not settle the intellectual questions. Head and heart had to be reconciled separately — which makes the testimony more credible, not less.

Huff “squaring up the dots”

Objection 8

Grace can save monsters

If Hitler, a mass-murderer, or Sammy the Bull repents, is he saved? The scandalon that drives some hearers away.

PBD interviewer

Strongest response

Embraced as the demonstration-feature

Strobel: “Yes. That’s exactly right… none of us is good enough.” The conversion of the least-likely candidate is the evidence of radical grace, not an embarrassment to be defended against.

Strobel grace-radicality

Objection 9

You’re a Christian by upbringing

Belief is inherited, not evidenced — you believe what you were raised in.

Standard sceptic

Strongest response

Worldview-change runs against upbringing

Lennox deliberately exposed his faith to its opposite for decades (the Marxist world; Peter Singer raised an atheist), watching worldview-change run against formation rather than with it. A reverse-Freud move.

Lennox reverse-Freud

Objection 10

Pure evidentialism — weigh it all first

Surely the rational thing is to study all the evidence before deciding whether to believe.

Implicit / rationalist

Strongest response

The 70-year-old student is “insane”

Craig (via Kierkegaard): faith cannot hold its breath while reason sifts forever. A loving God pursues the seeker; encounter (Pascal) is decisive, and evidence is ministerial.

Craig Kierkegaard + Pascal frame

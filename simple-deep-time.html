Deep time and the Bible

Fossils, dinosaurs, the Ice Age, and Genesis.

The rocks tell a long story. Fossils stacked through layers laid down over ages, dinosaurs that ruled the earth and vanished long before the first people, ice sheets that came and went. If the earth is really that ancient, what happens to Genesis? The short answer surprises a lot of people: far less than you would think.

First / No date in the text
Step 1 of 4
Does the Bible give an age?

The Bible never puts a date on the earth.

The whole clash depends on the idea that Scripture teaches a young earth of a few thousand years. Read it closely, and you find it never actually says so.

Show the verse
Cliffe Knechtle makes the point bluntly. He has read the Bible for over fifty years, and he challenges anyone to show him the verse that states how old the earth is, because there isn't one. If you want to know the age of the earth, he says, you turn to geology, the same way you turn to biology to learn about a tree, not to Genesis. The Bible simply never poses the question.
The genealogies
The famous figure of about six thousand years did not come from the text. It came from Archbishop Ussher in the sixteen hundreds, who added up the genealogies as though each named father came directly before his son. But Hebrew genealogies routinely skip generations to spotlight the important names, so son of can mean descendant of. The sums were never meant to be a stopwatch, and they never yielded a real date.
Before the days
And Lennox points to something in the grammar that most readers miss. The opening line, in the beginning God created the heavens and the earth, sits before the sequence of days begins, and in a different past tense. Whatever you make of the days themselves, the beginning is set at an indefinite time before them. The text itself leaves the age of the earth wide open.
The theory you'll hear
The Bible clearly teaches the earth is about six thousand years old. It says so nowhere. That figure is a seventeenth-century calculation by Archbishop Ussher, who added up the genealogies as if each were an unbroken father-to-son chain. But those lists skip generations on purpose, to highlight the names that matter, so the totals never produced a genuine age. The Bible names no age for the earth at all.
✓
Confidence: Scripture nowhere states the age of the earth. The young-earth figure is a later sum from genealogies never built to be added up, and Genesis 1:1 even places the beginning before the days. Deep time fills a silence rather than breaking a claim.
Second / Faith and the rocks
Step 2 of 4
Was it ever faith versus science?

Faith didn't lose a war to the rocks.

The story is usually told as religion digging in against the evidence of an old earth. The real history is messier, and a good deal friendlier.

A real range
Christians who fully trust the Bible hold a range of views. Some hold to a young earth. Many more accept the standard ancient earth and read the days of Genesis accordingly. Lennox, an Oxford professor of science and a committed Christian, says plainly that he has no difficulty at all with a universe of around thirteen and a half billion years, and that sincere believers simply differ on the matter.
Who built it
And remember who helped build the science. Several founders of geology and palaeontology were devout Christians. Nicolas Steno, who worked out the basic law of how rock layers are laid down, was a Catholic bishop. Pioneering geologists like Sedgwick and Buckland were Anglican clergymen. The notion that an ancient earth is an attack on faith is far more recent, and far more local, than people assume.
Not at war
So the battle line the objection draws, ancient rocks on one side and the Bible on the other, is largely a modern invention. The people who first read the layers of strata were not trying to topple Genesis. A great many of them were reading the rocks precisely as the handiwork of the God of Genesis.
The theory you'll hear
Religion has always fought science over the age of the earth. History tells a messier story. Some of the founders of geology and palaeontology were devout believers, including the Catholic bishop Nicolas Steno, who first explained how rock layers form. The idea that holding to an ancient earth betrays the faith is recent and narrow, not the ancient or universal Christian position.
✓
Confidence: Christians hold a genuine range of views, and most who engage the science accept an ancient earth. Several founders of geology were devout believers. The supposed war between faith and the age of the earth is far newer and blurrier than the objection assumes.
Third / What they actually are
Step 3 of 4
So what are they?

So what are fossils, dinosaurs, and ice?

With the age of the earth left open by the text, the three things in your question stop being threats and become simply the deep history of the world God made.

Fossils
Fossils are the long record of life on earth, the remains of living things pressed into rock and layered up over vast stretches of time. To a Christian who reads the rocks at face value, they are not a problem to explain away. They are the unfolding story of a creation that has been here far longer than we have, written into stone.
Dinosaurs
Dinosaurs were magnificent animals that lived, flourished and died out tens of millions of years before the first humans. That the Bible never mentions them is no stranger than the fact that it never mentions kangaroos or penguins. Genesis is not a catalogue of every creature. It is the account of who made them, and dinosaurs are among the most spectacular things he made.
The Ice Age
The Ice Age, when great sheets of ice advanced and retreated across the continents, is simply one long, cold chapter in the deep history of the planet. Like the fossils and the dinosaurs, it sits comfortably within the ages that the opening of Genesis quietly passes over, without ever once denying them.
The theory you'll hear
If dinosaurs were real and the Bible never mentions them, the Bible is incomplete. The Bible never mentions kangaroos, antibiotics or the Pacific Ocean either, and nobody counts that against it. It was never meant to be an inventory of everything that exists. It tells you who made the world and why you are here, and leaves the full list of creatures, living and extinct, to the world itself to reveal.
✓
Confidence: fossils are the deep record of life, dinosaurs are creatures that lived and died long before us, and the Ice Age is a chapter of the planet's long climate. None are mentioned or denied by Genesis, because Genesis is answering who and why, not the geological when.
Fourth / The bigger picture
Step 4 of 4
What Genesis is really claiming

Deep time makes the Maker bigger.

Look at what the opening of Genesis actually sets out to say, and you find that none of it rises or falls on the age of a rock.

What it claims
Genesis 1 makes a handful of enormous claims. There is one God, not a quarrelling pantheon. The world is made on purpose and called good, not flung out by accident. The sun, moon and stars are created things, not gods to be feared. And human beings carry the image of God, and real dignity with it. Those are the load-bearing claims of the chapter.
Untouched
Now notice this: not one of them depends on whether the earth is six thousand or four and a half billion years old. An ancient earth, a long fossil record, an age of dinosaurs and an Ice Age leave every one of those claims standing exactly where it was. The age of the rocks and the message of Genesis are simply answering two different questions.
Bigger not smaller
And here is the turn worth sitting with. A creation shaped over unimaginable ages, teeming with vanished wonders, does not make its Creator smaller. It makes him more staggering. The deep past is the long, patient backstory of the world Genesis says he spoke into being. The rocks do not bury God. They put his patience and his artistry on display.
The theory you'll hear
An ancient earth full of long-dead species looks nothing like a good creation. It looks like a long history, which is precisely what an old creation would have. Age is not the same as pointlessness, and the death of animals over deep time is not the moral evil the rest of the Bible is concerned with. A vast, ancient, teeming world is not less the work of a Creator. If anything, it is a great deal more.
✓
Confidence: the real claims of Genesis 1, one God, a purposeful and good creation, the stars as made things, humanity in God's image, are untouched by the age of the earth. Deep time does not shrink the Creator. It magnifies him.

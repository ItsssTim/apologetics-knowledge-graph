What counts as a “contradiction”?

A definitional question masquerading as an evidential one — and the place where four experts quietly hold four different doctrines of inspiration.

The question

When a critic alleges the Bible “contradicts itself,” what would actually count as a formal contradiction — and does inspiration require that no two manuscripts, accounts, or wordings ever differ?

Almost every popular “the Bible contradicts itself” objection trades on an unstated equivocation: between differentiation in detail (two true reports emphasising different things) and formal contradiction (A and not-A asserted of the same thing in the same sense) — and between the textual critic’s “error” (any divergence between two manuscripts) and the theologian’s “error” (a false claim about fact, doctrine, or practice).

The payoff is large but narrow. The experts do not argue that every discrepancy harmonises — several concede some do not. They argue that the category of “contradiction” is misapplied, that no doctrine rests on a contested verse or variant, and that inspiration locates the Word of God in the truthfulness of what Scripture teaches, not in the impossibility of scribal slips or authorial personality. The internal fault-line: precisely where to locate inspiration — and they do not all agree.

The argument — a cumulative case in four definitional streams

This is not a deductive syllogism. It is a cumulative case in which four converging streams each dissolve a different class of alleged contradiction. No single stream carries the load; any one answers only its own slice of the objection.

Stream A · Define your terms

  • A contradiction breaks the law of non-contradiction
  • A and not-A, same sense, same time
  • “Married bachelor” / “square circle”
  • Differentiation in detail is not contradiction
  • Carried by Huff, Gurry

Stream B · Two senses of “error”

  • Textual “error” = any inter-manuscript divergence
  • Theological “error” = a false claim
  • Eye-skip, dittography, word order, spelling
  • A scribe’s slip is “not God’s fault”
  • Carried by Huff / Mitchell, Gurry

Stream C · Genre & accommodation

  • Poetry and song are not failed science
  • Genesis 1 song; Genesis 2 narrative
  • “I am the door” is not woodenly literal
  • Metaphor is the more literal reading
  • Carried by Knechtle, Ward

Stream D · Variation as evidence

  • Independent witnesses should differ
  • Identical detail would signal collusion
  • Embarrassing + incidental detail marks history
  • Inerrancy protects the autographs; no doctrine hinges on a variant
  • Carried by Knechtle, McLatchie, Huff, Craig
∴ Converges

The threshold for “contradiction” is rarely met, the threshold for “error” is equivocated, and inspiration survives intact — because it was never a claim about copying fidelity or authorial uniformity.

The honest caveat: the experts hold four distinct settlements, not one. Knechtle affirms infallibility but not inerrancy; Ward relocates the Word of God to the person of Jesus and concedes “we don’t have the exact words”; McLatchie defends inerrancy of the autographs only; Huff and Mitchell hold God’s truthfulness untouched by imperfect copying.

How each expert deploys it

Wes Huff3 transcripts
The definition-first dominant voice
Distinctive moves
  • Define “contradiction” philosophically — breaks the rule of non-contradiction; most allegations are differentiation in detail
  • Women at the tomb — naming one woman capitalises on a detail; her plural speech implies more. A formal contradiction would be “Mary went” vs “it was Peter, not Mary”
  • Socrates parity — Aristophanes, Xenophon, Plato differ in detail; classicists give the author the benefit of the doubt. “NT scholars do the exact same thing”
  • Two senses of “error” (with Tim Mitchell) — a scribe’s spelling slip is “not God’s fault”; the tradition is self-correcting via marginal notes
  • Ehrman’s Mark variant — compassion vs anger is always between two readings, never blank uncertainty; Christology untouched
  • tota scriptura — no doctrine rests on one verse; footnotes are visible, “nothing is hidden”
Cliffe & Stuart Knechtle3 transcripts
Variation-as-evidence + genre, with a candid concession
Distinctive moves
  • Anti-collusion criterion — variation is positive evidence; identical detail is the tell of fabrication
  • Genre first (vs Smalley) — Genesis 1 song, Genesis 2 narrative; the historical-event-then-song pattern (Miriam, Deborah)
  • “I am the door” — a good literal reading honours metaphor; snake-handlers are caricatures, not mainstream evangelicals
  • Embarrassment + incidental detail (vs Apostate Prophet) — Peter’s denial; Simon of Cyrene’s named sons; 1 Cor 15:3-8 as a fact-check invitation
Concession — the corpus’s pivotal admission

Stuart concedes Joshua 10 only partly (“thank God for the Galileo types”) and offers no positive reading of Joshua 10:13. In audience Q&A he says: “I believe in infallibility and I don’t believe in inerrancy” — functionally a McLatchie-style inductive position, in tension with strict autographic inerrancy.

Jonathan McLatchie3 transcripts
Inerrancy of the autographs + the parity tu quoque
Distinctive moves
  • Inerrancy applies to the autographs only — recoverable by textual criticism; 1 John 5:7, John 7:53–8:11, Mark 16:9-20 footnoted; no doctrine hinges on them
  • Parity tu quoque — the corruption charge backfires: hadith of lost Qur’anic portions, 26 Arabic Qur’ans, no pre-1924 match, no autographs
  • Canonicity recognised, not imposed — apostolicity, orthodoxy, catholicity; Hebrews via apostolic association (Clement of Rome)
  • Synoptic-escalation rebuttal (vs Shabir Ally) — counter-examples run both ways (Mark 15:39 > Luke 23:47); high Christology is pre-Markan (Q, Phil 2 hymn of the 30s)
  • Paley’s undesigned coincidences — Acts × the Pauline corpus authenticate the letters
William Lane Craig1 transcript
The three-assumptions filter (vs Ehrman)
Distinctive move
  • The objection silently assumes three things — that the inconsistencies are (1) irresolvable rather than apparent, (2) at the heart rather than the periphery, (3) in sources of equal reliability
  • Each can be denied — “inconsistencies in a later less reliable source do nothing to undermine an earlier more credible source”
  • The multiply-attested core — crucifixion under Pilate, burial by Joseph of Arimathea, Mary Magdalene (“always named”) at the empty tomb, post-mortem appearances
Tactical hole — flagged honestly

Ehrman twice presses a clean yes/no — are the embellished later accounts historical or not? — and Craig does not answer. If unembellished accounts are more credible and embellished ones are also historical, the embellishment criterion “has no weight.”

Objections handled — cross-expert master list

Objection 1

The Gospels flatly contradict each other

Read horizontally, the resurrection accounts diverge on who went to the tomb, how many women, what was seen, the day and time of death.

Bart Ehrman; Apostate Prophet

Strongest response

Differentiation in detail ≠ formal contradiction

A contradiction breaks the law of non-contradiction. The core is multiply attested; peripheral divergence in a later, less reliable source cannot undermine an earlier, more credible one.

Huff non-contradiction testCraig three-assumptions filter

Objection 2

The Bible is full of “errors”

Textual critics count hundreds of thousands of variants. A Christian recoils from the word “error” applied to Scripture at all.

Bart Ehrman, Misquoting Jesus

Strongest response

Two technical senses of “error”

The textual critic’s “error” (any inter-manuscript divergence — eye-skip, word order, spelling) is not the theologian’s “error” (a false claim). “That’s not God’s fault, that’s a scribe’s fault.”

Huff / Mitchell definitional disambiguationGurry near-duplicate statement

Objection 3

Anti-scientific content discredits the book

A talking donkey, a talking serpent, the sun stopping in Joshua 10, a body floating upward in sight of all men. A God who wanted belief would not allow nonsense in.

David C. Smalley

Strongest response

Genre first — poetry is not failed science

Read literary style before hopping: Genesis 1 is song, Genesis 2 narrative; the historical-event-then-song pattern recurs (Miriam, Deborah). The Bible is not a science textbook that needs an “iPhone update” every two years.

Stuart Knechtle genre-sensitive reading

Objection 4

Differences prove unreliable eyewitnesses

If the Gospels were genuine eyewitness accounts, the vast contradictions and gaps would not exist.

Apostate Prophet; Ehrman

Strongest response

Variation is anti-collusion evidence

Identical detail is the tell of fabrication. Embarrassing detail (Peter’s denial) and incidental detail (Simon of Cyrene’s named sons) mark history, not fiction; 1 Cor 15:3-8 invites the reader to fact-check living witnesses.

Knechtle anti-collusion criterionMcLatchie / Huff embarrassment + onomastics

Objection 5

The text was corrupted (tahrif) — Christian scholars admit it

James White, Licona, even Craig concede the Bible was changed; Sinaiticus contains books absent from modern Bibles. The Qur’an is the only intact final revelation.

Aqil Onque (Muslim apologetic)

Strongest response

Autographs-only inerrancy + parity tu quoque

Variants are footnoted; no doctrine hinges on them; inerrancy applies to the recoverable autographs. And the charge backfires: hadith of lost Qur’anic portions, 26 Arabic Qur’ans, no pre-1924 match, no Qur’anic autographs either.

McLatchie autographic inerrancy + Qur’an parity

Objection 6

Christology evolved Mark → John, so John is unhistorical

Mark repudiates (“why call me good?”); Matthew softens it; only the latest Gospel, John, has “I am” and “the Father and I are one.” Time accounts for the escalation.

Shabir Ally

Strongest response

Counter-examples run both directions

Mark 15:39 (“Son of God”) is higher than Luke 23:47 (“righteous man”); Mark 14:62 (“I am”) more direct than Matthew/Luke. And the high Christology is pre-Markan — Q (Matt 11:27) and the Philippians 2 hymn, dated to the 30s.

McLatchie bidirectional counter-examples

Objection 7

We don’t have Jesus’ exact (Aramaic) words, so inerrancy is dead

Jesus spoke Aramaic; every manuscript is Greek; he wrote nothing down. What we have is treasured memories, translated and collected — not a verbatim record.

Keith Ward (Anglican)

Response — a relocation, not a rebuttal

Locate the Word in the person, not the words

Ward concedes the textual minutiae but relocates authority: the Word of God is the person of Jesus, witnessed (not transcribed) by the Gospels. Knechtle assents to personality-difference and contests only the literalism caricature — never defending high-view inerrancy here.

Ward person-of-Christ relocationKnechtle partial assent

Objection 8

Two true reports can’t both be literal — the stone rolled away

One Gospel says the stone had been rolled away; another that the women saw it rolled away. Both cannot be literally true.

Keith Ward

Gap — asserted, not exegeted

A harmonisation exists, but no expert walks it

The texts can be reconciled (Matthew: the angel rolls it before the women arrive; Mark/Luke: the women find it already rolled). The aggregator notes this; neither Knechtle nor anyone else actually exegetes it, so Ward’s premise stands unrebutted in the transcript.

Objection 9

Joshua 10:13 — the sun standing still

The sun stopping in the middle of the sky is “literally, physically impossible.” The single most-cited anti-scientific text.

David C. Smalley

Gap — conceded, not defended

No positive reading offered

Knechtle concedes the point (“thank God for the Galileo types who come around and fix the theology”) and offers no phenomenological, poetic, or otherwise positive reading of Joshua 10:13. The flagship objection is left undefended.

Aggregation gap

Objection 10

If all Scripture is God-breathed and God doesn’t change…

2 Timothy 3:16 + Malachi 3:6: if every text is inspired and God is unchanging, the misogyny of 1 Cor 14 / 1 Tim 2 still binds today. Two sets of Ten Commandments (Exodus 20 vs 34) are not the same.

David C. Smalley

Gap — not engaged

Left hanging

Knechtle acknowledges the frustration and pleads contextual reading but never answers the unchanging-God syllogism, the two-Decalogues claim, or the minor contradictions (colour of the robe). Stream C is gestured at, not deployed.

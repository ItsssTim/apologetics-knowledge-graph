What are the major apologetic methodologies?

The question

When a Christian sets out to defend the faith, where does the argument start — with neutral evidence, a prior proof of God’s existence, or the authority of Scripture itself?

This is the meta-question of §21: not which argument to run but what kind of move an argument is allowed to be. The corpus names four (sometimes five) schools — evidentialism, classical apologetics, reformed epistemology, and presuppositionalism, with cumulative case sometimes counted as a fifth — and the dispute is genuinely in-house: every speaker here is a confessing Christian arguing with other Christians about method.

The fault line is the myth (or reality) of neutral ground. Presuppositionalists deny any neutral standpoint from which an unbeliever may weigh evidence; evidentialists and classical apologists grant a shared rational starting point and reason “from the ground up.” Whether you may use the resurrection before proving theism, whether the unbeliever “borrows” logic, whether a transcendental argument is question-begging — all of it follows from where each school plants its first foot.

The convergent structure — a comparison of methods

This is not a syllogism the experts share; it is a taxonomy they argue over. The convergent content is the map itself — delivered most fully by McLatchie — and the decision it forces is binary at root: is there neutral ground?

School 1

Evidentialism — proportion belief to the evidence, “from the ground up.”

First move. Argue straight to Christ without a prior theism proof. Treats evidence as directly probative for specific Christian claims (the resurrection) with no theism precondition. Champion in corpus: Jonathan McLatchie (“a fairly staunch advocate”).

School 2

Classical — a two-step approach: prove God exists, then defend the resurrection.

First move. Establish bare theism first, because (the argument runs) a miracle makes no sense as a best explanation unless you have independent reason to think God exists. Champions: William Lane Craig, Richard Howe.

School 3

Reformed epistemology — the internal witness of the Holy Spirit is itself sufficient justification.

First move. Distinguish knowing Christianity is true (the Spirit’s witness) from showing others it is true (arguments do the second job). Champions: Alvin Plantinga (“the chief architect”); Craig also self-identifies here.

School 4

Presuppositionalism — begin from the authority of Scripture; there is no neutral ground.

First move. Transcendental argument from “the impossibility of the contrary” plus internal critique of the unbeliever’s worldview. Evidence is “God’s evidence,” meaningful only inside the Christian worldview. Champions: Cornelius Van Til, Greg Bahnsen, Scott Oliphant, James White (who rebrands it “lordship apologetics”).

The fault line

Is there neutral ground? Presuppositionalism answers no; the other three answer yes in varying degrees.

Cumulative case is sometimes counted a fifth school, but McLatchie denies it is distinct from evidentialism. Craig sits across two cells — a classical apologist who also identifies as a reformed epistemologist.

How each expert frames it

Jonathan McLatchie 2 treatments
The cartographer — evidentialist advocate & mapmaker
Signature moves
  • Delivers the four/five schools as a deliberate taxonomic ladder — lay of the land before critiquing one view. Irenic toward classical and reformed; unambiguous against presuppositionalism.
  • His own position: evidentialism — “proportion one’s beliefs to the available evidence,” “I begin from the ground up,” concluding not at bare theism but at Christ.
  • Carries verbatim Van Til (The Defense of the Faith) and Bahnsen (“reasonable in virtue of the impossibility of the contrary”).
  • Sharpest critique — the classical two-step is unnecessary: a low prior on a miracle “can in principle be overcome with sufficient evidence.”
Credibility move

Hostile-witness-on-himself: he was a presuppositionalist as an undergraduate (“when I began university 2007”) and was argued out of it. A former insider testifying against the position — and he pre-empts the “weak spokesman” complaint by conceding Van Til wrote poorly and Frame was a poor Four Views representative.

James White 1 treatment
Presuppositionalism as “lordship apologetics”
Signature moves
  • Theology determines method: a general defends differently depending on the object — and the Christian object is the triune creator, not bare theism.
  • The myth of neutrality: no “pretended place of neutrality” outside the lordship of Christ. Romans 1 — the unbeliever already knows God and suppresses it.
  • Borrowed capital: the unbeliever “borrows” logic and reason with no grounding for them in his own worldview — “no stealing from god’s world.”
  • Argues transcendentally from the impossibility of the contrary; uses evidence, but never granting the rebel sinner the right to judge God.
Tactical tell

Reframes rather than answers when pressed on Bahnsen–Stein — deflecting “how did Bahnsen move from theism to Christianity?” by insisting Bahnsen never moved: he started from triune theism. Signature opener: 1 Corinthians 1:18ff.

William Lane Craig 1 treatment
The classical critique of presuppositionalism
Distinctive moves
  • Occupies two cells: a classical apologist who also identifies as a reformed epistemologist, holding the know/show two-track distinction.
  • Classical presuppositionalism (Van Til, Clark) begs the question: it merely invites you to step into the worldview and then declares it true.
  • The Bahnsen exception: the transcendental-argument variant is “very amendable” to classical apologetics — it can be absorbed as a species of natural theology.
Stephen Meyer 2 treatments
Inference to the best explanation as the operative method
Distinctive moves
  • IBE is comparative and provisional: infer the “cause now in operation” (Lyell) known from repeated experience to produce the effect — replaceable by a better candidate.
  • Not a god-of-the-gaps: a positive inference, not an argument from ignorance.
  • The real disagreement is philosophy-of-science deep: Matheson’s inductive faith that materialism always wins. Meyer turns the track-record on itself — “materialism has explained everything but the really interesting stuff.”
  • Deploys a hostile-witness vignette: Shermer conceding the multiverse reply is “kind of lame.”
Wes Huff 1 treatment
Methodology as the demarcation line
Distinctive moves
  • Makes method itself the whole argument against Billy Carson’s ancient-astronaut oral histories: “what methodologies are you using to confirm that the orality is accurate?”
  • All ancient history — the Bible included — was originally oral, so the question is method, not orality.
  • Verisimilitude and probability gradients: historical analysis is IBE; internal evidence plus external multivalent corroboration.
  • The demarcation: without falsification and corroboration, “you don’t have history — you have a collection of stories.”
Signature posture

Concede everything concedable (“I would grant that ancient people are far more technological advanced”; “I’m not aware of that particular case” on the Dogon / Sirius B), refuse to dismiss the empirical question, and hold the methodology line. “I don’t know” as a legitimate apologetic answer.

Objections handled — cross-expert master list

Objection 1

Presuppositionalists reject evidence / cannot use it

If there is no neutral ground and the unbeliever has no right to weigh facts, the presuppositionalist seems barred from using evidence at all.

McLatchie & Howe, pressing White

Strongest response

All evidence is “God’s evidence”

Presuppositionalists gladly use evidence — but only as God’s evidence, meaningful inside the Christian worldview, never in a way that grants the rebel sinner the right to sit in judgement of God.

White “presuppositionalists are evidentialists”

Objection 2

The classical two-step is unnecessary — you must prove theism before the resurrection

A miracle makes no sense as a best explanation unless you have independent reason to think God exists; so theism must be established first.

Classical school (Craig, Howe)

Strongest response

A low prior can be overcome by evidence

No prior theism proof entails a zero prior on a miracle; even a low prior “can in principle be overcome with sufficient evidence.” Prophecy, the trilemma, and Paul’s conversion independently raise the prior on a Jesus-specific miracle.

McLatchie evidentialist rebuttal

Objection 3

Classical presuppositionalism begs the question

Van Til and Clark invite you to step into the Christian worldview, accept its presuppositions, and then see if it makes sense of reality — with no argument for doing so.

Craig, against Van Til / Clark

Strongest response

Reject the core, absorb the Bahnsen variant

The classical-presuppositional core is simply “assume our view is true and then show it’s true.” But Bahnsen’s transcendental argument — that God is a necessary condition for trusting our rational faculties — is amenable to classical apologetics as natural theology.

Craig split-the-school move

Objection 4

Materialism always wins eventually — just wait for the naturalistic explanation

Every time we wait long enough, a materialistic explanation emerges; so we are entitled to expect one for the origin of biological information too.

Stephen Matheson

Strongest response

The track-record fails on the deepest questions

Origin of information, fine-tuning, the beginning of the universe, and consciousness all resist materialistic explanation. “Materialism has explained everything but the really interesting stuff.” The disagreement is philosophy-of-science deep, not biological.

Meyer track-record reversal

Objection 5

The design inference is a god-of-the-gaps / argument from ignorance

Inferring design just dresses up present ignorance — “we can’t explain it yet” — as a positive conclusion.

Standard naturalist

Strongest response

IBE is positive and provisional

IBE infers a known cause of the effect in question, not the mere absence of an alternative; and it is provisional — if a cause “as good or better” came along, “we’d have to look at that.”

Meyer criterion / method

Objection 6

Rival eyewitness/oral testimony is just as good (Dogon, Hopi, pleiadian)

Every culture has eyewitness accounts — star-beings, underground origins — that are “100,000% opposite” of the Bible. The Dogon knew Sirius B’s orbit before modern telescopes.

Billy Carson

Strongest response

Oral testimony is not history until a method converts it

“What methodology are you using to confirm that the orality is accurate?” Without falsification and internal/external corroboration you have stories, not history. The gospels clear that bar; flying somewhere and asking the locals does not.

Huff demarcation by method

Objection 7

“You picked a weak spokesman” (straw presuppositionalism)

Critiques of presuppositionalism lean on poor representatives — Van Til’s opaque prose, Frame’s weak Four Views chapter — rather than the strongest form.

Anticipated in-house complaint

Conceded, then answered

Names the problem before the opponent can

McLatchie concedes Van Til “was not the best writer in the world” and Frame was a poor representative — but maintains the core arguments are still answered on their merits. Honest, though it leaves the strongest TAG form under-engaged.

McLatchie fairness concessionpartial

Objection 8

“How did Bahnsen move from theism to Christianity?” (the transition problem)

A transcendental argument might get you to bare theism, but how does it get you specifically to Trinitarian Christianity?

Raised in the Bahnsen–Stein roundtable

Gap — deflected, not answered

Reframe rather than rebuttal

White insists Bahnsen never “moved” — he started from triune theism. This dissolves the question rather than supplying a positive bridge from theism to the Trinity. No worked transition is offered anywhere in the corpus.

Aggregation gap

Persistent gaps — what the corpus does not answer

Gap 1

Howe’s ontological-vs-epistemological equivocation

Presuppositionalists slide between “God is the precondition of intelligibility” (which classical theists also affirm) and “the presupposition of God is the precondition of intelligibility” (which they deny).

Richard Howe

Gap — never engaged

White does not address it

The deepest unanswered objection in the node. White reframes the question rather than meeting the equivocation charge head-on.

Aggregation gap

Gap 2

Is the transcendental argument itself neutral-ground reasoning?

If Bahnsen’s TAG can be absorbed into natural theology (as Craig holds), the presuppositional/classical distinction the school depends on collapses.

Implied by Craig’s absorption move

Gap — unanswered by presuppositionalists

No reply to the absorption

No presuppositionalist in the corpus answers Craig’s claim that their strongest argument is really just natural theology in disguise.

Aggregation gap

Gap 3

Reformed epistemology is described but never defended

The “internal witness of the Spirit as sufficient justification” claim faces an obvious de jure objection — how does private warrant bear on public dispute?

Standard epistemic challenge

Gap — no inside defence

Accurately mapped, never stress-tested

McLatchie describes Plantinga’s school correctly, but no extraction defends or pressure-tests it. The Dogon / Sirius B datum (Huff concedes ignorance) and the cumulative-case-as-distinct-method question are likewise left open rather than resolved.

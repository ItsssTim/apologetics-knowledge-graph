How to share
Bible-loving, and one question away

How do I share the gospel with a Jehovah's Witness friend?

Jehovah's Witnesses are sincere, disciplined, Bible-loving people who will happily talk about God for hours. That love of Scripture is your bridge. Almost everything turns on a single question they have been taught to answer differently from the rest of the church: who exactly is Jesus?

First / Honour the Scripture
Step 1 of 4
Honour the love of Scripture

Start where you agree, then find the hinge.

A Witness is not someone who needs convincing that God matters; they have given their life to it. Begin on the ground you genuinely share, then move gently to the one question that changes everything.

Common ground
Start with what you genuinely share: a high regard for the Bible and a real desire to please God. Your friend reads Scripture, prays, and orders their whole life around obeying him. That is not nothing. Honour it. You are not talking to a sceptic; you are talking to a serious believer who loves the same book you do.
The hinge
The one question everything turns on is the identity of Jesus. Almost every other difference flows downstream of this single point. Get clear, kindly, that this is the hinge, and you avoid a hundred scattered arguments. Keep coming back to it: not the calendar, not the blood transfusion policy, but who Jesus actually is.
What they hold
In Watchtower teaching Jesus is Michael the archangel, a created being, a god but not God almighty. Almost everything else follows from that one answer. Understanding this is vital, because when you say Jesus is Lord, your friend hears something far smaller than you mean. The whole conversation lives or dies on this question.
The mistake to avoid
Skipping past the common ground straight into conflict. If you open swinging, your friend simply raises the defences they have been trained to raise, and the real question never gets a hearing. Honour the love of Scripture you share first. That earned trust is what lets you reach the one point that matters.

Remember: begin on the Scripture you both love, then move gently to the one question that matters, who Jesus really is.
Second / Who is Jesus
Step 2 of 4
Who is Jesus, really?

Walk the texts gently, in order.

Since you both love the Bible, let the Bible do the work. Open it together and walk through the texts slowly, not as ammunition, but as a path you travel side by side.

He is God
Thomas falls before the risen Jesus and calls him my Lord and my God, and Jesus accepts it rather than correcting him (John 20:28). John opens by saying the Word was God (John 1:1). If Jesus were only an exalted creature, this is the moment to say so; instead he receives the worship. Let your friend sit with that.
The divine name
Jesus takes the divine name to himself, saying that before Abraham was, he is (John 8:58), echoing the very name God gave at the burning bush. And all things were made through him and hold together in him (Colossians 1:16-17). The one through whom everything was created cannot himself be one of the created things.
Worshipped
Even the angels are told to worship him (Hebrews 1:6). Yet Scripture allows worship of God alone. So a created angel, Michael or any other, could never qualify for what Jesus is given here. Gently ask your friend: if only God may be worshipped, and the angels are commanded to worship the Son, what does that make the Son?
The mistake to avoid
Firing verses like ammunition instead of walking through them together. A rapid volley of proof-texts feels like an ambush, and your friend has been drilled to deflect exactly that. Slow down. Read one passage, let it breathe, ask what they make of it. You are showing them something, not scoring points off them.
✓
Remember: let the texts do the work, gently and in order; you are showing, not winning.
Third / The rest grace gives
Step 3 of 4
The rest that grace gives

Offer the rest their system cannot give.

Behind the doctrine there is usually a tired heart. The gospel has something to offer your friend that the organisation never can: rest, and the assurance of being saved.

The weight
Many Witnesses live under works and the organisation, never quite sure they have done enough. There are hours to log, meetings to attend, a standard always just ahead of them. Beneath the confident answers is often a quiet, exhausting uncertainty about whether they will finally measure up. See that weary heart, not just the arguments.
Assurance
The Bible offers something their system withholds: assurance. John writes so that you may know you have eternal life (1 John 5:13). Not hope it, not work towards it, but know it. For someone taught that their standing is never quite settled, the idea that you can actually know is almost too good to believe.
A gift
Salvation is a gift, not wages earned (Ephesians 2:8-9). It is by grace, through faith, and not of works, so no one can boast. To someone carrying an exhausting uncertainty, that is deep relief: the debt is not theirs to pay down, because it has already been paid in full for them. Offer them that rest.
The mistake to avoid
Debating doctrine while missing the weary heart in front of you. It is easy to win every point about the Trinity and never once notice the person is tired. The deepest thing you have to offer is not a better argument; it is rest. Lead with the gift of grace, and let the doctrine serve the person, not the other way around.
✓
Remember: offer the rest of grace, not another burden.
Fourth / How to carry it
Step 4 of 4
How to carry it

Questions and patience, never an attack.

How you carry the conversation matters as much as what you say. The goal is to win your friend, gently and over time, not to win a round.

Ask and listen
Ask gentle questions and listen, rather than ambushing. Questions invite your friend to think for themselves, which a lecture never does. What do you make of this verse? What first drew you in? Curiosity disarms in a way that confrontation cannot, and it tells them you actually see the person, not a target.
No proof-text war
Do not get dragged into a verse-by-verse battle over their New World Translation, and never open by attacking the Watchtower organisation. That only hardens the very defences they have been trained to raise, and the conversation turns into a contest nobody wins. Stay off the well-worn battlegrounds and keep returning to the person of Jesus.
Keep pointing
Keep pointing, kindly and repeatedly, to who Jesus is and to the rest he gives. You may have the same gentle conversation many times before anything shifts, and that is fine. Be the steady, loving friend who is still there long after the first talk, praying more than pressuring, and trusting God with the timing.
The mistake to avoid
Opening with an attack on the organisation, or a proof-text war on the translation. Both harden them, because both confirm exactly what they have been warned to expect from outsiders. Lay down those weapons. Aim past the system, at the friend, and keep the focus on Jesus and on grace, where the real invitation lives.
✓
Remember: you want to win your friend to the real Jesus, not to win an argument about the Watchtower.

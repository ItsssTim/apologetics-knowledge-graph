The question

Has the meaning of the Bible been lost or distorted by the long chain of translation and copying between the original authors and the modern English reader?

This is the transmission worry in its most common form: “you can’t know what the Bible originally said, because it has been translated so many times.” It is the objection Joe Rogan reaches for, the objection the campus skeptic reaches for, and – in mirror image – the objection the King James Only and Textus Receptus Only movements reach for from inside the church.

The stakes are mostly defensive. Q-4.4.3 does not prove a doctrine; it clears an obstacle so the historical and textual arguments can be heard at all. Its strategic value is one transferable distinction – translation is not transmission – that defuses the skeptic and the KJV-Only believer in a single stroke.

The convergent argument — evidence streams, not a syllogism

This is not a deductive proof but a cumulative defeater assembled from converging lines, all pointing to the same verdict: change is real, but it is updating, not corruption. The corpus lands on confidence – neither “we’ve lost the original” nor “every word and comma is perfect.”

Stream A · The category correction

  • Translation (across languages) ≠ transmission (copying inside a language)
  • The “telephone game” silently confuses the two
  • Huff · Knechtle

Stream B · Direct sourcing

  • Modern English is translated directly from Greek and Hebrew
  • Not down a chain of intermediate languages
  • The originals are not discarded
  • Huff · Knechtle

Stream C · Clarity, not corruption

  • English changes (“awful” once meant “full of awe”)
  • The 1611 KJV is now unintelligible (1 Chron 28:16)
  • Updating the rendering preserves meaning
  • Huff

Stream D · Committee guarantee

  • Multi-denominational committees produce major translations
  • No faction “gets to the controls”
  • Institutions steward trust no individual can verify
  • Mark Ward (with Huff)

Stream E · Zoomed-out agreement

  • Read several major translations: the story is identical
  • Joseph in Genesis, Jesus in the NT – the same
  • Differences are stylistic, not doctrinal
  • Mark Ward

Stream F · The counter-example

  • A genuine corruption does exist: the JW New World Translation
  • Material removed and added – a “perversion”
  • Proves the category is real and the majors are not in it
  • Preston Perry

How each expert deploys it

Wes Huff4 transcripts
The translation-vs-transmission distinction
Signature moves
  • Translation ≠ transmission. Transmission is scribal copying within a language (“same to same”); translation is rendering across languages (Greek into Latin, “like what Jerome did”). Correct the category before engaging the substance.
  • Named foil – Joe Rogan. Quotes the actual recent episode (“hebrew to the greek into the latin into the german…”) rather than a strawman.
  • Two extremes to avoid: radical skepticism (“translated so many times”) and absolute certainty (KJV-Only). The latter collapses on one question – which translation? (1611 vs 1769 vs 1873).
  • 1 Chronicles 28:16 – the 1611 KJV “at par bar westward” is unintelligible; the NIV is clear. Change is updating, not corrupting.
  • Erasmus / KJV steelman – honours the KJV as scholarship done with ~7 manuscripts, then notes the earliest papyri (last 150 years) reveal no new reading.
Mark Wardwith Huff · 1 transcript
The institutional-trust argument
Distinctive moves
  • Committee guarantee – multi-denominational committees mean “nobody gets to the controls”; no Bible is “the Calvinist one” or “the Arminian one.”
  • Institutional trust – the asphalt-municipality analogy: we rely daily on institutions we never personally verify.
  • Zoom out – read the majors with an open mind and they tell “exactly the same” story.
  • The Socratic TR trap – if you love the Textus Receptus, why reject the NKJV and MEV, which are also TR-based? “I get a lot of bluster and non-answer.”
  • Frames the dispute as a Romans 14 conscience issue; credits the healthy intuition (inspiration attaches to the originals).
Huff adds — TR genealogy

The Textus Receptus is not monolithic: five Erasmus editions → Stephanus → Beza → Scrivener’s 19th-century edition, reverse-engineered to match the KJV translators’ choices. “A Greek version of an English version of a Greek version.”

Cliffe Knechtle1 transcript
The one-step Aramaic correction
Signature deployment
  • Concrete recitation – challenged with “do you know what he really said?”, he recites John 14:6 verbatim, turning an abstract worry into a textual fact.
  • One-step correction – concedes Jesus spoke Aramaic but corrects the inference about the text: the NT was written in Greek. One fewer link than assumed.
  • Greek lingua franca – “Alexander the Great made sure that Greek was the common trade” language of the known world.
  • Personal credential: “I did study the Greek.”

Objections handled — cross-expert master list

Objection 1

The Bible is a telephone game

Translated so many times that, like a whispered message down a line of people, the meaning is hopelessly garbled by the end.

Joe Rogan; popular skeptic

Strongest response

Confuses translation with transmission

The analogy is wrong on its own terms (whisper rules, a single chain) and factually wrong about method: modern English Bibles are made directly from the Greek and Hebrew, not copied down a chain of languages.

Huff category correction

Objection 2

It went Hebrew → Greek → Latin → German → French → English

Each translator worked from the previous language, so the English is many removes from anything original.

Joe Rogan

Strongest response

That chain does not exist

It describes a transmission chain that simply is not how modern translation works. Translation is direct from the originals; the intermediate-language succession is imaginary.

Huff direct-sourcing fact

Objection 3

You’re reading English – you can’t know what Jesus said

Your knowledge of Jesus’s words is mediated through an English translation of earlier sources.

Knechtle’s campus interlocutor

Strongest response

Recite it, then correct the premise

Knechtle quotes John 14:6 verbatim to show he does know; then corrects the assumption – the NT text is Greek, not Aramaic, and he has studied the Greek himself.

Knechtle concrete recitation

Objection 4

He spoke Aramaic – that gap loses the meaning

Jesus spoke Aramaic; translating across that gap means we cannot recover what he actually said.

Knechtle’s interlocutor

Strongest response

One fewer link than assumed

Knechtle concedes Jesus spoke Aramaic but the authoritative text is Greek – the lingua franca via Alexander the Great. The chain is shorter than the objection imagines.

Knechtle linguistic correction

Objection 5

My Bible is perfect in every word and comma

The KJV-Only / “absolute certainty” position: the English I hold is exactly, perfectly the Word of God.

King James Only movement

Strongest response

Which KJV?

There is no single KJV – the 1611, the 1769 Blayney, and the 1873 revisions all differ. The 1611’s 1 Chron 28:16 is unintelligible today; the NIV is clear. Change is updating, not corrupting.

Huff “which translation?” dilemma

Objection 6

The Textus Receptus is the pure Greek; critical texts are corrupt

The Greek behind the KJV preserves the authentic New Testament; Nestle-Aland is a corrupted modern reconstruction.

TR-Only / KJV-Only

Strongest response

The TR is not monolithic – and you reject the NKJV

Five Erasmus editions → Stephanus → Beza → Scrivener’s reverse-engineered text (“a Greek version of an English version of a Greek version”). And if you truly love the TR, why reject the NKJV and MEV, which are also TR-based?

Ward Socratic trapHuff TR genealogy

Objection 7

A faction biased the translation

Complementarians / Calvinists / dispensationalists slanted the text toward their theology.

KJV-Only adjacents

Strongest response

Nobody gets to the controls

Multi-denominational committees prevent any one camp from steering. No Bible tracks a denominational fault line; the one allegedly “monkeyed with” place was revised back to the King James standard in the 2025 edition.

Ward committee guarantee

Objection 8

A non-Greek-reader can’t vet any translation

If you can’t read the originals, you can’t confirm any English Bible is faithful – so you can’t trust one.

Posed by Huff to Ward

Strongest response

God ordained this situation

Almost no one in history has been a native speaker of both biblical Hebrew and Koine Greek, so the whole church has always met Scripture in translation. “You’re going to have to trust somebody. And God is the one who gave us this situation.”

Ward theology of trust

Objection 9

The NWT is just another modern translation

The New World Translation is no different from the NIV or ESV – JWs call it “the most accurate Bible throughout the whole world.”

Jehovah’s Witnesses

Strongest response

A perversion, not a translation

A translation renders the source faithfully; the NWT has material removed and added, altering the propositions of the underlying text – so “it is no longer a translation.”

Perry categorical distinction

Objection 10

Fine – but the chain is inside the Greek

The Greek itself is reconstructed from thousands of variant manuscripts, so the “telephone” worry simply relocates into the transmission of the Greek text.

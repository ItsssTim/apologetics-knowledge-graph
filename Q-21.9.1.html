The hostile-witness tactic

Win the case not with your own evidence, but with the concession your opponent cannot accuse you of manufacturing — their own scholar, their own movement, their own theology.

The question

When is it most persuasive to win a case with the opponent’s own words rather than your own?

Q-21.9.1 is not a doctrine but a tactical pattern. A hostile witness is a source that is not on your side — an opponent, a skeptic, an enemy attestation — whose own admission nonetheless supports your conclusion. Because that source has every incentive to say the opposite, the concession carries disproportionate evidential weight. It cannot be dismissed as Christian bias.

The corpus treats the tactic at three scales: the textual (Ehrman concedes in his own appendix), the movement (a former anti-theist disowns New Atheism on air), and the diagnostic (a witness is only hostile to a strawman — fix his theology and the hostility evaporates).

The shared structure — one tactic, three scales

Q-21.9.1 is a method, not a syllogism. Every deployment runs the same three moves — identify the adverse source, extract the admission against interest, then let the concession do the work — instantiated at a different scale by each expert.

Scale A · Textual (Huff)

  • Source: Bart Ehrman
  • Admission: appendix concedes no essential belief turns on a variant
  • Documented: the Q&A was deleted from later printings
  • Sting: “what did I just read”
  • Named: “good Bart / bad Bart”

Scale B · Movement (Knechtle)

  • Source: Greydon Square, ex-anti-theist
  • Admission: anti-theism was “angry,” not rational
  • Concedes: religion a “net positive”
  • Technique: let him hold the floor
  • Probe: “you can admit it”

Scale C · Diagnostic (Lennox)

  • Source: Stephen Hawking
  • Diagnosis: he “confuses god with the gods”
  • Refinement: hostile only to a strawman
  • Flip: understanding increases awe
  • Payoff: false dilemma dissolves

John Lennox1 transcript
The diagnostic refinement — hostile only to a strawman
Distinctive moves
  • Does not extract a concession; supplies the logic underneath the tactic.
  • Hawking “confuses god with the gods” — the primitive god-of-the-gaps, “not the concept that lies at the heart of any monotheistic religion.”
  • The biblical God is “the god of the whole show” — not even the deists’ god who “lit the blue touch paper” and retired.
  • Signature flip: understanding increases awe — you admire the jet-engine designer because you grasp the engineering, not because you don’t.
  • Payoff: a witness is only hostile to the position he actually targets. Fix the theology and the false dilemma (God or physical law) collapses into a category error.

Objections handled — cross-expert master list

Objection 1

“Ehrman’s facts are simply correct — the variants are real.”

The manuscripts do contain hundreds of thousands of variants. Ehrman is a credentialed textual critic; you cannot dispute his data.

Bart Ehrman, Misquoting Jesus

Strongest response

Facts conceded; interpretation disputed

“Usually Bart’s facts are correct but his interpretation of them is often not correct.” And Ehrman’s own appendix concedes that essential Christian beliefs are unaffected — so the data cuts the other way.

Huff + McLatchie appendix concession

Objection 2

“You’re cherry-picking a stray line from the appendix.”

Quoting one isolated sentence against the whole thrust of a book is selective and unfair.

Skeptical reader

Strongest response

The suppression is itself documented

It is not a stray line but a dedicated editor-and-author Q&A — so damaging the publisher deleted it from later printings because it hurt sales. The inconsistency is now a physical fact about the editions.

Huff publishing-history detail

Objection 3

“Indoctrination of children is the real harm of religion.”

Whatever its other merits, religion does damage by indoctrinating children before they can reason — the standard New Atheist charge.

Greydon Square’s former movement; Dawkins

Strongest response

Every worldview proselytises

Atheism, agnosticism and apathism all transmit themselves to the next generation. The charge proves nothing distinctive about religion — and Square concedes the point on air.

Stuart Knechtle Square agrees

Objection 4

“Atheists arrive by reason; theists by emotion.”

The New Atheist self-image: unbelief is the cool, rational default and belief is the emotional comfort blanket.

New Atheist self-image

Strongest response

The witness himself reverses it

“A lot of atheists think, oh, I came to this by reason and logic — no, no, you were angry like I was.” Self-disclosure from inside the movement beats any external assertion.

Knechtle extracted from Square

Objection 5

“Science has steadily displaced God — God is just the gaps.”

Every advance in physics shrinks the territory left for God. The God hypothesis is a placeholder for ignorance, replaceable by scientific advance.

Stephen Hawking; Dawkins

Strongest response

That is god-of-the-gaps, not monotheism

The biblical God is “the god of the whole show” — of what we understand and what we don’t. The more we understand, the more we admire the designer. Hawking is rubbishing a concept no monotheist holds.

Lennox diagnostic refinement

Objection 6

“Religion is at best harmless, at worst poisonous.”

Religion poisons everything; on balance it has been a net negative for humanity.

Hitchens, god is not Great; New Atheism

Strongest response

The corpus’s own hostile witness disagrees

A former anti-theist rates religion “at least a neutral — and in some ways I could make a strong argument that it’s been a net positive for most of mankind.” The concession comes from the opposing side.

Knechtle Square’s concession

Objection 7

The substantive New Atheist arguments are never engaged

Dawkins on theodicy, Hitchens on Old Testament morality, Harris on revelation — winning a man’s biography is not refuting the movement’s case.

Dawkins, Hitchens, Harris

Gap — not addressed

Biographical concession is not refutation

The content arguments are gestured at as Square’s former influences but never argued on the merits. A hostile witness disowning New Atheism is not the same as New Atheism being wrong.

Aggregation gap

Objection 8

The appendix concession may prove too little

No essential doctrine turning on a variant still leaves the longer ending of Mark, the woman caught in adultery, and 1 John 5:7 in play — places Ehrman presses hard.

Internal textual-criticism literature

Gap — not addressed

Scope of the concession unexamined

The corpus does not map where Ehrman would still object, so the appendix concession is narrower than the rhetorical framing implies. Nor does anyone reflect on whether “even your own side admits it” can mislead — a hostile witness can be mistaken in your favour.
